HO'S BOOTLEG TAVERN IN THE PRESS

This dive bar savior is set on letting you "party like it's 2010" with pre-housing-crisis prices.

Amy Coppermna in Thrillist

Go for the bacon plate or the butter burger. Or, better yet, the off-menu Top Ramen with chicken, nori and egg. It’s only available after midnight. Which makes an impossible amount of sense.

The Weekender Urban Daddy

The best part is that the food is available late night, something the neighborhood is sorely lacking.

Stephanie Tuder in Eater SF

Should you require something a little more adventurous, you can always order the IceBerg—an apt name for a blended margarita floating in beer.

Lovely folks at Urban Daddy

Ho’s Bootleg Tavern is the creation of Masaye Waugh and Jerrid Wood. This is the first restaurant/bar venture for the pair, but both Waugh and Wood are local industry vets who met while working at Tonic in Russian Hill. Most recently, Waugh could be found at Skylark in the Mission and Wood at Bullitt on Polk Street.

Sarah Fritsche in SF Gate

Ho’s Bootleg Tavern has just decided to let you eat brunch there. Brunch that involves things like bottomless mimosas and Bloody Marias, stuffed french toast and bacon-egg-and-cheese-stuffed potato skins. Because they care.

The Weekender Urban Daddy

Ho's Bootleg Tavern: providing what San Franciscans need and want: affordable thrills.

Masaye Waywiser in INDIEGOGO
